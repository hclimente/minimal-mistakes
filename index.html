I am a PhD student at Center for computational biology which is a joint research unit involving Mines ParisTech, Institut Curie and INSERM. The center is dedicated to epidemiology, bioinformatics and computational systems biology of cancer. I am co-supervised by Chloé-Agathe Azencott and Véronique Stoven.

Research interests

The big question of my thesis is the [missing heritability problem](http://hclimente.eu/wisdom/notes/bio/missing_heritability.html), the discordance between the estimated genetic determination of a phenotype, and the actual genetic factors we find in association with it. The community has come up with several hypothesis behind this problem, and during my thesis I intend on exploring two of them: [epistasis](http://hclimente.eu/wisdom/notes/bio/epistasis.md) and the high p, low n curse. To do so, I am carrying out [GWAS](http://hclimente.eu/wisdom/notes/bio/gwas.html) which include a priori knowledge, namely biological networks that give a context to every SNP. This approach allows for context-dependent statistics, where we can fine tune the significance threshold for each individual SNP. Currently we are applying these approaches to [familial breast cancer](http://hclimente.eu/wisdom/notes/bio/familial_brca.html).
